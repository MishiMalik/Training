HOW DO OTHERS PERCEIVE YOU?

Global studies have highlighted the importance of executive presence to career success

For instance, in one survey of 400 corporate executives and CEOs:

89%

said executive presence helps people get ahead

78%

said limited executive presence can hold people back

Executive presence is primarily about influence and impact. Leaders who have it are seen as confident and engaging. They’re known for building strong relationships across organizational levels. They’re perceived as calm and decisive in a crisis. They communicate clearly and persuasively. And they mobilize the right people to tackle complex challenges. In short, they get results.

Over 20 years of coaching leaders, we’ve identified a comprehensive list of traits, skills and behaviors associated with executive presence, and we’re excited to share this research with your organization.

HOW YOUR LEADERS CAN BENEFIT FROM A STRONGER EXECUTIVE PRESENCE

We provide individualized guidance on cultivating a compelling executive presence and leadership brand that drives success. Count on us to help your people — from high-potential managers and first-time leaders to leaders in new roles and executives at all levels — grow in areas such as:

  • Increasing self-awareness: Get to the bottom of the question, "How am I perceived"?

  • Looking the part: Enhance personal presence and credibility by focusing on voice, grooming, dress and body language.

  • Maintaining a positive presence: : Understand how to avoid the consequences of a dark executive presence.

  • Building new strengths and overcoming challenges: Develop necessary capabilities and eliminate blind spots to succeed in any role.

  • Regulating emotions under pressure: Stay poised and composed in times of heightened stress, and remain flexible and adaptable in response to uncertainty.

  • Developing political savvy: Build strong relationships throughout the organization by fostering and leveraging one's network and managing up, down and across.

    • Inspiring and influencing others: Communicate with people across all levels in a clear, persuasive and nonintimidating manner to win confidence, generate enthusiasm, gain buy-in and effect positive change.
